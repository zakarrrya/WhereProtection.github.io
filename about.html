Why is Gun Control Good/Bad?

Well, for gun control in the USA, it mainly revolves around the Second Amendment. The United States’ gun culture originates from its history of colonialism, expansion and revolutionary cause as well as the Second Amendment. The Amendment gives a proclamation of which it states: “A well-regulated militia, being necessary to the security of a free State, the right of the people to keep and bear Arms, shall not be infringed.” Supporters of gun control laws have voiced that the Amendment solely be for militias – in hopes to minimize gun violence; that restricted guns have and do exist, and that most US citizens are for gun control. Yet adversaries of gun controls believe the Amendment preserves a citizen’s right to claim possession of guns – that guns are a need for any potential threats that tarnish them and their portrayal as a true American.

When were Gun Control Laws Introduced?

As accompanied by the rule of the Second Amendment in the United States, the right to possess guns was introduced to the United States Constitution within the Bill of Rights, on December 15th 1791. Gun control laws were adopted in the pursuit of securing gun violence and limiting the mistreatment of arms. Citizens that endorsed the laws for gun control debated that each state containing bearers of arms should be affiliated with a military service serving as part-time soldiers, and as proclaimed by the Second Amendment, these military groups were “well-regulated” (Gale, 2018), undergoing the process that of which concerned: training, firearms and recurring drills.

The National Firearms Act of 1934 was introduced through supplementary rules issued by the Federal Firearms Act of 1938. The next crucial gun law was established in 1968 when Congress delivered the Gun Control Act in relation to the assassinations of civil rights leader Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. and Senator Robert F. Kennedy. The law stopped home-delivered distribution of all arms and ammo and also stopped the production of guns to felons, the mentally ill, illegal drug users, soldiers dismissed from the forces and fugitives.

Although this law was efficient at preventing inappropriate use of guns from certain individuals this did not prevent the ones that still had access to them. Fast forward to the 00s, even more gun control laws had been produced; restrictions went forward from the mid-late 90s when the government prohibited the production of assault weapons and from 2004 the ban expired when Congress was unable to prolong the ban, which led to mass shootings increasing - the lack of conformity on gun laws had caused a vast majority of laws being erected in terms of registration for guns. In Massachusetts, California and New Jersey there are permits required in order to invest in arms. Some states such as Utah allow citizens to carry a gun in public. Advocates of gun rights have complained about the flawed legislations being “too restrictive”.
