How Far Can Gun Laws/Gun Violence Go?

As well as gun control laws in the US, there are other countries that operate the use of gun maintenance – Canada has an estimated 30.8 guns per 100 people and a homicidal rate of 5.1 per 1 million people; the guns are obtainable to the population but have maintained crucial limitation on alternated guns and who can buy them. The UK (although gun violence is very uncommon) has an estimated 6.2 guns per 100 people for owned arms and a homicidal rate of 0.7 per 1 million people and maintains some of the strictest gun laws within the West, as handguns are generally banned. In South Africa, guns are somewhat accessible and widely owned – they have gun schools for people aged 12+ and are recommended gun lessons.

The question is: how much further can this all really go? Do we need guns in order to survive or is it a superiority complex? Gun violence must stop, and it’s best that we stick to restricted gun laws that can simultaneously satisfy adversaries who aren’t all in favour of gun laws. By reducing firearm access to youth, holding the gun industry accountable and warranting oversight over the distribution of gun violence, we may be able to save lives and prevent gun violence as a whole.
