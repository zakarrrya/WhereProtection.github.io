Gun Laws & Violence: In Summary

The current gun control system in USA revolves around profit, possession, security and accountability of producing and owning guns. The central importance around gun control laws mean that guns are licensed and monitored through the process of purchasing and other forms of transactions; people who purchase guns can be those who feel the need to purchase a firearm for their own sense of security or protection, such as farmers etc., however others (mainly criminals) use it for power or revolt. Most recent laws present permitted enquiry in order to own a firearm further outlining the need to formulate a strategic proposition and prohibition for malicious use of guns.




The practice of gun use nowadays is primarily associated with police or military force, as well as for crimes. Recent surveys define gun-owners at a high number of 120.5 arms for every 100 civilians residing within the US. Given this amount it suggests, from these surveys, that inflated rates of owned firearms are commonly related to high homicidal numbers, accidental gun deaths and suicide. In addition, the easy access of arms (especially illegal) have led to high levels of violent crimes, and that most civilians have access to these guns or carry them on their person, means that it is imperative to observe the predisposition of actions around how these guns are used. This is especially relevant in reference to the amount of mass shootings that have increased over the recent years.
