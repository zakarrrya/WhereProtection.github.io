Who Has Been Affected/Inflicted By Gun Violence?

Gun violence is a very controversial present-day human rights issue and has an impact on everyday lives, affecting citizens around the globe. Anyone can be distressed by gun violence but unreasonably impacts POCs (People of Colour), women, LGBTQ+ members and other groups who are disparaged. A study of gun owners produced by researchers from Harvard University in 2015 discovered that purchases considered for around one-fifth of gun distribution were among those surveyed. Inconsistent reporting has totalled in an inadequate catalogue lacking in crucial data or information within many categories such as domestics and mental health areas. In 2007 a college student shot and killed thirty-two people at Virginia Tech University. The student had been instructed to attend mental health treatment by a Virginia Judge prior to this incident, however under state law was not barred from buying the arms used in the shooting.

In 2014, a shooting that took place in Marysville Pilchuck High School in Washington, D.C., by a fifteen-year old student who shot and killed four other students with a gun he had stolen from his father, which was secured through a licensed dealer.

Inconsistent reporting became a recurring issue within some states along the years that had passed.

Did You Know?

The first ever gun originates from 10th century China, known as Chinese Fire Lances. These guns were constructed of metal tubes or bamboo which protruded shrapnel and flames at a target, and then later appeared and were adapted in Europe around the 12th century.

Two years later, on June 12, 2016, an armed gunman shot and killed forty-nine people at a gay nightclub in Orlando, Florida, before being killed by law enforcement. This shooter had been previously interrogated by the FBI for connections to terrorism, however was legally allowed to purchase the arms used in the attack.

The Las Vegas shooting was perhaps the worst of them within recent US history. A sixty-four year-old Nevada resident Stephen Paddock had shot from the thirty-second floor of the Mandalay Bay Hotel at an open-air music festival which contained 22,000 people. He killed himself as police searched for the room he resided in, containing twenty-three guns. More than 500 people had been injured from the attack, and fifty-eight people were killed in the process. Stephen had used bump stocks which allows the semi-automatic rifle to rapidly fire ammunition similarly to a fully automatic rifle.

Bump stocks are not common with most Americans and advocates have called for these devices to be banned. The devices have been described as “not difficult to make” and “could be produced on a 3-D printer”. Amongst school shootings recurring over the years, and public places too, it’s a wonder of how much longer these shootings can continue for much longer before humanity is satisfied with the desecration of innocent lives.
